Breadth-Thrust Signal

Risk-on conviction meter — the bullish mirror of the Equity Defense Dashboard. A grouped four-dimension breadth score with a conditional forward-return study measured against a bootstrap baseline.

How to use this

  • Read the number as conviction, not a trade. A high score means breadth has thrust — many names participating in a turn up — not that today is an entry point. It sizes how much the tape confirms a risk-on turn.
  • Lean on the six-month median, not the headline win rate. On this 2018–2026 sample the durable edge is the lift in six-month median return. The win-rate edge only clears the baseline noise band at the looser ≥1/≥2 thresholds (see the lift table); the ≥3 win rate sits inside the band. Do not treat score ≥ 3 as a confirmed standalone trigger.
  • Respect entry discipline. Thrusts are most useful deployed after flat or negative stretches, to avoid buying the peak of an already-strong run — not to chase one.
  • Combine, do not isolate. This is the bullish mirror of the Equity Defense Dashboard. Use it to gauge risk-on conviction alongside your defensive signals, not as a lone timing model.
  • Mind the caveats. Eight years is a thin sample (few fresh events); 111 of 715 former members are unfetchable and drop from history; pre-2018 is excluded. Treat conclusions as suggestive.
